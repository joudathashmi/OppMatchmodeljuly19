AI-Powered Company × Opportunity Matching Framework

Scalable, Explainable, Sector-Aware, and Self-Validating
Step 1
Preprocessing
  • Cleans company and opportunity text: lowercasing, noise removal, normalization.
  • Standardizes unstructured content for embedding-based comparison.
  • Strips corporate boilerplate and price or geography noise so capability meaning dominates.
Step 2
Sector Ontology Expansion
  • Expands sector tags using a domain-specific sector tree and token canonicalization.
  • Ensures broader coverage across related sub-domains.
  • Example: "information and communication" → ICT, telecom, network, hardware.
Step 3
Sector Scoring & Cross-Sector BridgesCHANGED from hard filtering
  • Does not hard-filter: every pair is scored, so valid cross-sector matches are never dropped.
  • Score from exact match, industry family, or a structured bridge (Industrial to ICT, Chemicals to Pharma).
  • A bridge needs 2+ non-generic capability terms, blocking spurious links.
Step 4
Semantic Embedding & SimilarityUPGRADED model
  • Uses text-embedding-3-large for 3,072-dimension vectors (was ada-002 / 1,536).
  • Captures contextual meaning of profiles and opportunity descriptions.
  • Cosine similarity, then percentile normalization for comparable scores across runs.
Step 5
Product / Service MatchingCHANGED fusion
  • Separately embeds the products and services text for an added, product-level signal.
  • Combined score is a weighted blend (profile 30, product 30, sector 25, evidence 15), not a max.
  • Goal: detect deeper product-level alignment, not only profile overlap.
Step 6
GPT-Based Validation, Graded & VotedUPGRADED
  • Asks: can this company realistically deliver or supply this opportunity?
  • Graded verdict, not yes/no: Direct (can execute), Partial (supplier or partner), None.
  • Self-consistency: sampled 3x, majority wins; agreement reported as confidence.
Step 7
Abstention & Evidence GuardrailsREPLACES soft match
  • Pure semantic similarity with no sector link no longer forces a match (it caused false positives).
  • Cross-sector value is captured through Step 3 bridges and the Step 6 gate instead.
  • If nothing is validated Direct or Partial, the opportunity abstains: "no validated fit".
Step 8
Ranking and Scoring
  • Ranks matches by the blended final score (top rank = 1).
  • Good Fit and High Fit require a GPT verdict, so labels never overstate.
  • Goal: an honest shortlist for business teams, with a verdict, confidence, and rationale.
